What Are Servant Events?

Servant Events are flexible ministry experiences that combine faith, fellowship, and hands on service in a way that helps participants grow spiritually while making a real impact.

More Than Just Completing a Task

Servant Events at Heit’s Point are not simply about getting a job done. They are designed to help students step into lives of Christian service by putting their faith into action in visible and meaningful ways. Some events may last longer and involve multiple service opportunities, while others may be shorter and centered on a specific need or project.

No matter the length, each Servant Event is built around the same mission: to serve with willing hearts, learn through God’s Word, and grow through shared Christian community. Participants are invited to work hard, worship together, reflect on what God is teaching them, and see firsthand how service can bless both the people being served and the people serving.

These experiences give students the opportunity to slow down, focus on others, and live out the love of Christ in practical ways. They leave with more than memories. They leave with a stronger sense of purpose, deeper faith, and a clearer understanding of what it means to follow Jesus.

The Purpose of Servant Events

Servant Events exist to help young people grow as disciples who understand their faith, care deeply for others, and are ready to serve wherever God calls them.

  • To connect faith with real action
  • To build Christ centered friendships
  • To develop leadership through service
  • To meet real needs with willing hands
  • To help students live out God’s love

Hands On Opportunities

Potential Service Projects

Service projects at Heit’s Point can look different from event to event depending on the season, the needs around camp, and the gifts of the group serving. Some projects may focus on outdoor work, others on upkeep, improvement, cleanup, or preparation for future ministry. While the tasks themselves may seem simple at first, each one plays a meaningful role in caring for a place that is used to share God’s Word, build Christian community, and welcome people into rest, worship, and fellowship.

This is part of what makes Servant Events so important. Students are able to see that faithful service is not limited to big moments or highly visible roles. God works through willing hands, steady effort, and humble hearts. Whether someone is cutting grass, painting a building, picking up rocks, clearing brush, or helping prepare spaces for camp and retreat guests, that work becomes part of something bigger than the task itself. It becomes an act of love and stewardship offered in service to the Lord and to others.

In this way, even ordinary work points us back to Christ. It reminds us that the gifts He has given us are meant to be used faithfully, that serving others is one way we live out His love, and that no task done for His glory is wasted. Every project becomes an opportunity to grow in faith, bless others, and take part in the ongoing ministry of camp in a real and lasting way.

Why It Matters

Why These Projects Matter

The work students do during Servant Events is meaningful because it supports real ministry in real ways. These projects are not random tasks added just to stay busy. They help care for the spaces, grounds, and facilities that are used for worship, camp life, retreats, fellowship, and the sharing of God’s Word throughout the year.

They Meet Real Needs

Every project helps support the ongoing ministry of camp. Whether students are clearing brush, painting, cleaning, preparing buildings, or improving outdoor spaces, their effort directly helps make Heit’s Point safer, more welcoming, and more ready for ministry.

They Build Character

These projects help students grow in responsibility, perseverance, humility, and teamwork. They learn how to work hard, stay faithful in ordinary tasks, and serve with a good attitude even when the work is challenging.

They Teach Faithful Service

Students begin to see that serving the Lord is not only about big public moments. Faithful service often happens in practical, steady, and sometimes unseen ways. These projects help students understand that every act of service done in love has value.

They Support Future Ministry

The work completed during Servant Events helps prepare the camp for future guests, campers, retreats, and ministry opportunities. Students get to see that their service has a lasting impact beyond the event itself.

More than anything, these projects help students experience the joy of being useful in God’s kingdom. They leave knowing their time, effort, and gifts can truly make a difference for others and for the ministry happening at Heit’s Point.

Servant Events are shaped by a whole person approach. We want students to grow in what they understand, what they value, and how they live out their faith in the world around them.

That is why Head, Heart, and Hands is such an important part of the Servant Events experience. It helps connect learning, spiritual growth, and action into one meaningful rhythm of discipleship.

  • Heart helps students care deeply

H

Head

Learning and Understanding

Head is about helping students grow in understanding. Through Bible study, discussion, and reflection, participants learn why Christian service matters and how God’s Word shapes the way we see the people and needs around us.

This part of Servant Events gives students a foundation. It helps them understand that service is not random good behavior. It is a faithful response to the grace and love we have received in Christ.

  • Bible study and devotion time connected to service
  • Conversations about calling, faith, and Christian responsibility
  • A clearer understanding of why we serve and who we serve for
H

Heart

Faith and Character

Heart is about the inner life of faith. Servant Events help shape students to be compassionate, humble, joyful, and attentive to others. As they worship, reflect, and serve, they begin to see more clearly how God is shaping their character.

This is where service becomes personal. Students do not just complete a task. They begin to care more deeply, notice people more intentionally, and grow in the kind of love that reflects Christ.

  • Growing compassion and care for others
  • Building humility, patience, and encouragement
  • Strengthening faith through worship, prayer, and reflection
H

Hands

Action and Service

Hands is where faith is put into action. Students actively serve through practical projects, meaningful work, and opportunities to help meet real needs. This is a visible expression of Christian love lived out in everyday life.

Whether they are helping at camp, serving in ministry settings, or supporting others through hard work and teamwork, students experience the joy that comes from giving their time and energy for something that matters.

  • Helping with projects that support camp, ministry, and community needs
  • Working together with purpose, responsibility, and teamwork
  • Learning that faithful service is something they can carry into everyday life

Using Our Gifts to Serve

Servant Events help students recognize that the gifts God has given them are meant to be used faithfully in service to others. Whether someone is strong with physical work, encouraging to others, dependable in leadership, or willing to help with whatever is needed, every gift has a place in ministry.

These events create space for students to discover that service is not only about ability. It is about faithfulness, willingness, and a heart ready to serve the Lord with zeal and joy.

When students work together in this way, they see how God can use ordinary people and ordinary tasks to accomplish meaningful work for His kingdom.
